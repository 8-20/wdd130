Wyoming

Wyoming was the 44th state to be admitted to the United States on July 10, 1890. However, it was the first state to grant women the right to vote. Although it is the 10th largest in area, it is the smallest in population with slightly more than 500,000 people. Wyoming is famous for its national parks including Yellowstone and Grand Teton National Parks and for its abundance of outdoor activities such as hunting, fishing, skiing, hiking, and mountain climbing.
